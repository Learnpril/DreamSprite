Hey! If you're just dipping your toes into AI art (or coming back after a break in 2026), welcome. This quick guide is your map - no deep dives yet, just the big picture so you know where everything fits.

The modern AI art pipeline is basically: **Idea -> Prompt/Control -> Generate -> Refine -> Output** (2D sprite, 3D asset, concept art, etc.). You can start super simple (cloud tools like Midjourney) and level up to full local control (ComfyUI).

## The Basic Pipeline Flow

1. **Ideation** - Brainstorm what you want (character, scene, style). Use real sketches, mood boards, or even AI to spark ideas.
2. **Prompting & Control** - Write descriptive text + use tools like ControlNet for poses, depth, etc.
3. **Generation** - Run through a model (checkpoint) to create the image.
4. **Refinement** - Upscale, fix hands/faces, add details via img2img, or pipe to 3D tools.
5. **Output** - Save, post-process in Photoshop/Krita/Blender, export for games/site/blog.

Pro move: Document every step in Obsidian/Markdown so you can recreate wins later.

## Free vs Paid Tools in 2026

You don't need to spend a fortune to make great stuff - especially with local options.

### Free / Low-Cost Starters

- **Leonardo.ai** - Still one of the best free tiers (150 daily tokens ~30 high-quality images/day, public gens). Great for photoreal/anime, Flux models, easy web interface.
- **Google ImageFX / Imagen 3** - Generous daily limits, photoreal strength, no signup hassle.
- **Stable Diffusion + ComfyUI (local)** - Completely free/unlimited once installed. Runs on your GPU. Download models from Civitai/Hugging Face.
- Other free-ish: Ideogram (limited daily), Playground AI, self-hosted Flux/SDXL.

### Paid Power Tools

- **Midjourney** - Cloud-based, Discord/web, amazing coherence. Plans (as of March 2026):
  - Basic: $10/mo (~200 fast images, no unlimited relax).
  - Standard: $30/mo (15 fast GPU hours, unlimited relax).
  - Pro: $60/mo (30 hours, stealth mode for private gens).
  - Mega: $120/mo (60 hours, max concurrency).
    Great for quick ideation, but outputs are public unless Pro+.


My take: Start free with ComfyUI install. If you love cloud speed, grab Midjourney Basic for a month to test!

## Mindset Tips for Beginners (Don't Burn Out!)

AI art is addictive but frustrating at first - here are some tips to keeping your sanity:

- **Experiment fast, fail cheap** - Generate tons of junk. Every bad image teaches you something (prompts, samplers, etc.).
- **Master fundamentals first** - Learn composition, lighting, anatomy basics. AI is a tool - good art sense makes it 10x better.
- **Iterate, don't chase perfection** - Use img2img to refine instead of starting over. Save seeds/prompts that work.
- **Community > solo** - Join r/StableDiffusion, Civitai comments, ComfyUI Discord. Share WIPs - feedback accelerates learning.
- **Take breaks** - AI can feel like a slot machine. Step away, sketch traditionally, or just vibe. Burnout kills creativity.
- **Document everything** - Prompt, settings, model version, why it worked/failed. Turn your notes into reusable guides.

You're not "cheating" with AI - you're using a new medium. Focus on the story/emotion you want to convey, and the tech will catch up.
